About

The rapid boost in next generation sequencing technologies has increased the sizes of databases. In fact, with these new technologies, scientist are opened to new possibilities and definitely new challenges. What used to be a problem of lack of data, has now become a problem of how to store these data and how to extract the information burried deep within. Thanks to organisms like Arabidopsis thaliana and a group of others known as model organisms, plant biologist have had a more insights as to how the genetic material of plants drive everything we see from germination to anthesis and to fruit.

If we take a step back to look at the DNA, it is amazing how all of the diversity seen in the world in encoded by four nucleotides efficiently repeated by nature to varrying lengths. A computer programmer may be tempted to say the genetic code is the easiest code to write, but it remains the most difficult code decrypt. From the discovery of transposable elements by Barbara McClintock to the invention of the CRISPR gene editing technologies, much effort has been directed towards linking genetic diversity to genetic code.

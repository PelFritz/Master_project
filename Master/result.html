Results

In the OR-type, we set the rules for an OR system. We mutated two motifs this time: the TATAAA and a CATAAT motif. The TATAAA was mutated into a TGTAAA and the CATAAT was mutated into CACAAT. Sequences can be expressed if they contain any of the mutations or both of the mutations. In the first image, a gene had the mutation on the CATAAT element. So we see the negative orange signal.
In the second image the gene tested had the mutation on the TATAAA element hence the green signal shown.
Finally a gene with the mutations on both elements, will have a mixture of green and orange signals which are distant from each other. At this point you may be wondering why we have two orange signals and just one green signal. All these signals are produced from the same gene, however these are different variants of the same gene because they come from different genomes. This means there is possibility of indels changing elements or shifting them within a sequence.

Motif discovery: We are also able to capture some important motifs using our CNNs. We could actually capture the GATA-disc promoter motif within the CBL interacting protein kinase 9, a gene involved in biosynthesis in A. thaliana CNNs are very powerful because of their repetitive use of filters. Since these filters can be predefined as the length of short motifs, we simply create filters which are 6 bp long and use these to scan an entire sequence. As the filters go through each sequence, specific mathematical calculations called convolutions will be performed. This produces feature maps at the end of each convolutional layer. One can think of a convolution as inputs multiplied by the weights plus biases. The weights are represented by our short windows which are 6 bp long. Once we train our network and do predictions we can feed these predictions to DeepLIFT which will help us visualize the regions which were crucial to prediction. The GATA-disc for example is represented by the pattern "TGATAA". This is captured within our predictions.

Hint The beautiful images and interpretation of our deep convolutional network we have is all thanks to the friendly folks who created the feature importance extraction tool called Deeplift. Deeplift computes importance scores by comparing each original sequence to a refernce input sequence, in our case a shuffled form of the original sequence.
